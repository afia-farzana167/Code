Sugar plays a major role in dental health by fueling the growth of harmful bacteria in the mouth. When sugar is consumed, bacteria convert it into acids that erode tooth enamel, leading to cavities and decay. Frequent consumption of sugary foods and drinks increases the risk of tooth damage, as prolonged exposure to acid weakens enamel over time. Reducing sugar intake, alongside regular brushing and flossing, is key to preventing cavities and maintaining overall oral health.
